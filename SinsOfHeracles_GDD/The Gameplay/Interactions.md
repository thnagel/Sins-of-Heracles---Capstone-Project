## **Player Interactions**

### **Overview**
Players interact with the world and its inhabitants to manage progression, gather resources, and enhance their capabilities.  
Key interactive systems include **villagers**, **merchants**, **blacksmiths**, **shrines**, and **the king**, each providing distinct gameplay functionality.

---

### **Villagers**
Villagers provide **world-building and lore** through conversations and small interactions.

- Serve as ambient NPCs to make towns and settlements feel alive  
- Offer snippets of story, background on locations, or hints for exploration  
- Some villagers may give small side quests or direct players to hidden areas  
- Interaction is primarily **optional but enriching**, adding depth to the world  

---

### **Merchants**
Merchants offer consumables, equipment, and temporary buffs in exchange for **Ichor**.

- **Consumables**  
  - Single-use healing items to restore health  
  - Throwing knives or other ranged consumables  
  - Temporary buffs for damage, stamina, or defenses  

- **Trading Mechanics**  
  - Players spend Ichor to acquire items, providing an alternative use for currency besides leveling  
  - Merchants may have **tiered inventories** that expand as players progress or defeat bosses  
  - Rare consumables or unique items may appear under specific conditions, encouraging exploration  

---

### **Blacksmiths**
Blacksmiths allow players to **craft and upgrade weapons and armor**.

- **Crafting**  
  - Requires materials collected from enemies or exploration  
  - Unlocks new weapons or gear for different playstyles  

- **Upgrading**  
  - Enhances existing equipment with improved damage, defense, or special modifiers  
  - Encourages investment in favorite gear instead of constant replacement  

- **Player Choice**  
  - Players can choose between upgrading multiple items or specializing in a single powerful weapon  
  - Interaction fosters strategic planning in resource allocation  

---

### **Shrines**
Shrines serve as key points for **checkpoint management and progression**.

- **Saving & Respawning**  
  - Act as checkpoints; dying elsewhere respawns the player at the nearest shrine  
  - Restore healing resources such as *Gifted Ambrosia*  

- **Leveling Up**  
  - Use Ichor to allocate points into core stats (Strength, Dexterity, Vitality, Endurance)  
  - Encourage strategic decision-making regarding which stats to enhance  

- **World Connection**  
  - Shrines may unlock fast travel points or new NPC services  
  - Serve as gameplay anchors, rewarding exploration and progression with a safe space

---

### **King Eurystheus**
King Eurystheus serves as the **main quest giver** for Heracles.

- Assigns the **labors of Heracles**, providing the primary narrative and gameplay goals  
- Interacting with him triggers story progression and unlocks new zones

---

### **Interaction Philosophy**
The interaction systems are designed to:

- Reinforce **exploration** and engagement with the game world  
- Provide **meaningful choices** in resource allocation (Ichor, materials)  
- Allow players to **customize playstyle** through gear upgrades, consumables, and stats  
- Support **storytelling and world-building** through villagers, quests, and major NPCs  
- Integrate progression systems (crafting, leveling, and combat) into the world in a cohesive, intuitive way  

By connecting combat, progression, exploration, and narrative, player interactions remain impactful and engaging throughout the game.
